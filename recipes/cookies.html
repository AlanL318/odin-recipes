Cookies

Description

“It all started back in 1939. Ruth Wakefield, who ran the successful Toll House restaurant in Whitman, Massachusetts, was mixing a batch of cookies when she decided to add broken pieces of Nestlé Semi-Sweet chocolate into the recipe expecting the chocolate to melt. Instead, the semi-sweet bits held their shape and softened to a delicate creamy texture and the chocolate chip cookie was born.

Ingredients

  • Butter
  • Brown sugar
  • Sugar
  • Eggs
  • Vanilla Extract
  • Flour
  • Baking Soda
  • Salt
  • Nestle Toll House Semi-Sweet Chocloate Morsels

Preperation

  1. Preheat oven to 375
  2. In a mixing bowl, beat butter, brown sugar, sugar, and vanilla
  3. Add eggs
  4. Fold in flour and stir together
  5. Stir in chocolate morsels
  6. Drop onto parchment paper lined baking sheets by rounded tablespoon.
  7. Bake for 9 to 11 minutes or until light golden brown
